"(Creative Coding) is a process, based on exploration, iteration, reflection and discovery, where code is used as the primary medium to create a wide range of media artifacts."

"process"

Creative programming can be compared to the creation of a spontaneous painting, in which the artist has given little or no concrete thought to the final result. It is a picture that emerges in the moment, through the play of shapes and colors. The conscious separation of the creative act from the final motif is characteristic of Creative Coding. In Mitchell and Bown's definition, this is clarified in the first sentence with the term "process." It is distinguished from the term "method," which always involves an intention.
